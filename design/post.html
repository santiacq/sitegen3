Why I switched to Neovim (again)

Every couple of years I end up back here. I try VS Code, I try Zed, I try whatever the new thing is. And every time I come back to Neovim. This time I think I understand why.

The problem with GUIs

It's not that graphical editors are bad. They're fine. But there's a friction I can never shake — the mouse. Every time my hand leaves the keyboard, I lose a small thread of thought. Over a full day, that adds up.

I know you can use Vim keybindings in VS Code. I've tried. It's not the same. The emulation is always slightly off, and the mental model doesn't fully transfer.

What changed this time

Two things made the difference: lazy.nvim for plugin management and the LSP ecosystem finally being mature. Setting up a working config used to take a weekend. Now it takes an hour.

Here's the core of my config: 

-- init.lua (simplified)
vim.g.mapleader = " "

require("lazy").setup({
  "neovim/nvim-lspconfig",
  "nvim-telescope/telescope.nvim",
  "nvim-treesitter/nvim-treesitter",
})

That's it. Three plugins and you have fuzzy finding, syntax highlighting, and language server support. Everything else is optional.

Will I switch again?

Probably. But for now this setup feels right. It's fast, it's minimal, and it stays out of my way. That's all I really want from an editor.
